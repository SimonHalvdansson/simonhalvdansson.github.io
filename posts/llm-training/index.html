Training a good LLM in five minutes

By Simon Halvdansson | Sep. 2025

Over the past few years there has been many innovations in LLM design; smart pretraining recipies, substantial posttraining RL, and architectural improvements. In this post, we'll look at how these architectural improvements translate to small models trained for short amounts of time. If you're making a truly optimal model constrained to a certain size, something like Gemma 3 270M is a better place to look. The recent post What's the strongest AI model you can train on a laptop in five minutes? by Sean Goedecke was a strong inspiration for this post but my aim here is to iterate more on the model.

Figure: Want a scatter plot of preplexity for different models here.

Starting from a GPT-2-type model, we will work through the following set of techniques to see what affects validation preplexity on the TinyStories dataset:

  • Dropout
  • Pre/post norm
  • FFN archtecture (SwiGLU)
  • LayerNorm / RMSNorm
  • Mixed-precision (AMP)
  • Wide/deep blocks
  • Grouped Query Attention (GQA)
  • Optimizers (Muon)
  • Schedulers
